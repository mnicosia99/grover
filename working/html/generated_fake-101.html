A prototype urine-based urine exfoliative system developed by a team of researchers from the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology of the University of Cincinnati, the North Africa Center of Infectious Diseases at the University of California, San Diego, the University of Adelaide and the University of Florida is a cost-effective method for the treatment of fatal, persistent gut-bacteria infections such as methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) and Staphylococcus aureus (Staph).
